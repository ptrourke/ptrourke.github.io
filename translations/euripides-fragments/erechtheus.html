Fragment 360N2 / 50 A

A noble act pleases all mankind.
But action after the time is past,
that would be ignoble.

I will surrender my daughter to death.

I can count many reasons for this sacrifice:
I make this sacrifice for the city,
and there is no better city than this one.
We are not aliens who have traveled here
from some far-away land, but were born in this
country, on this ground. In our day and age
people move about like pegs on a makeshift
game board, transplanted to different cities
as nails are driven into a piece of lumber --
and though they call themselves citizens,
they do not act the part.

There is a reason for us to bring up children
-- so that one day they may shield the homeland
and the altars of the gods.
